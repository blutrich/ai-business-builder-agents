# ClimbClaw: Phase Diagnosis & Honest Coaching

## Where You Are Right Now

You are in **Phase 4: Monetization** -- and you are not done with it yet.

Here are the facts:
- 3 paying customers at $29/month = **$87/month MRR**
- 2 months live
- No indication of strong product-market fit signals (referrals, retention patterns, easy sales conversations)

The framework says you need **5 paying customers minimum** before you even think about growth channels. You have 3. But more importantly, 3 customers in 2 months is a signal you need to pay attention to, not paper over with ad spend.

---

## The Mistake You Are About to Make

You want to run Instagram ads and start a YouTube channel. I need to stop you here because **this is one of the most common and expensive mistakes at your stage.**

The skill framework is explicit about this:

> **Avoid for now:** SEO, content marketing, YouTube. These take 6-12 months to produce results. You need feedback now.

> **Paid ads:** Can work but targeting is harder early on.

Instagram ads and YouTube are **Phase 5: Growth** tactics. You are not in Phase 5. Jumping to growth tactics before you have product-market fit is like pouring gasoline on a campfire that hasn't caught yet -- you will burn through cash and have nothing to show for it.

Here is what will actually happen if you execute your current plan:

1. **Instagram ads**: You will spend $500-2,000+ testing audiences, creative, and copy. With only 3 customers, you do not yet understand your buyer well enough to target effectively. Your cost per acquisition will be brutal. You will likely spend more acquiring a customer than their $29/month lifetime value justifies.

2. **YouTube channel**: You will spend 10-20 hours per week scripting, filming, editing, and posting climbing training tips. Results take 6-12 months to materialize. Meanwhile, you are not talking to customers, not improving the product, and not figuring out why only 3 people out of every person you have talked to decided to pay.

Both of these are distractions from the real work you need to be doing right now.

## What You Should Actually Do (Phase 4 Priorities)

### 1. Diagnose Why Only 3 Customers in 2 Months

This is the critical question. Before spending a dollar on ads, answer these:

- How many people have you pitched ClimbClaw to in total?
- What is the conversion rate from pitch to paying customer?
- Of the people who said no, why did they say no?
- Are your 3 paying customers actually using the product regularly? What does retention look like?
- Have any of your 3 customers referred anyone? If not, why not?
- What did those 3 customers say that was different from the people who did not buy?

**Action:** Book a 15-minute call with each of your 3 paying customers this week. Ask them: What made you decide to pay? What is the single most valuable thing ClimbClaw does for you? What almost made you not sign up? Would you recommend this to a climbing partner, and if not, why not?

Use an AI note-taker on these calls, then feed the transcripts to an AI assistant and ask it to find patterns.

### 2. Get to 5 Paying Customers Through Direct Outreach

Do not use ads. Use direct, personal outreach. This is what works at your stage.

**Actions:**
- Identify climbing communities where your ideal customer hangs out: climbing gyms (local Facebook groups, Discord servers), Reddit communities like r/climbing and r/climbharder, Mountain Project forums, climbing-specific Slack/Discord communities
- Do personalized outreach to climbers who match the profile of your 3 existing customers
- Use any outreach/prospecting approach -- even manual DMs on Instagram to climbers (this is different from running ads; it is direct, 1-to-1 communication)
- Offer a limited-time early adopter discount (50% off, so $14.50/month) to reduce friction
- Target just 2 more paying customers to hit your first 5

**The goal is not scale. The goal is learning.** Each conversation where someone says yes or no teaches you something. Ads do not give you that feedback loop.

### 3. Iterate Aggressively on the Product

With only 3 customers, you have a tiny sample but a golden opportunity: you can treat each one like a VIP and learn exactly what they need.

**Actions:**
- Collect feedback after every interaction
- Be brutally honest about what features they actually use vs. what you built
- If all 3 customers use one specific feature and ignore the rest, that is your signal -- double down on that feature
- Do not be afraid to change your target customer, your pricing, or even your core feature

### 4. Watch for Product-Market Fit Signals

The framework is clear: when you find the right combination of customer + product + business model, you will see these signals:

- Retention improves (customers stick around month over month)
- Referrals start happening organically
- Sales conversations get easier -- people "get it" quickly
- You start hearing the same positive feedback from multiple customers

**You do not have these signals yet with 3 customers in 2 months.** That is okay, but it means your job is to find them, not to scale what you have.

## When You CAN Start Instagram Ads and YouTube

You graduate to Phase 5 (Growth) when:

1. You have at least 5 paying customers (ideally 10+)
2. Your existing customers are retained -- they are not churning after month 1
3. You understand exactly who your buyer is and why they buy (not guessing, knowing from conversations)
4. Sales conversations have gotten noticeably easier
5. At least 1-2 customers came from referrals

When those conditions are met, the framework recommends this Growth Trifecta:

1. **Personalized outreach at scale** -- systematize what already works using outreach automation tools (Apollo, Instantly, Lemlist, Clay, or similar)
2. **Organic content** -- start posting on the platform where your customers spend time (Instagram could work for climbers, but only after you know your message)
3. **Paid retargeting** -- take the people who engaged with your organic content and retarget them with ads on Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, etc.

Notice the order: outreach first, organic second, paid third. And paid is specifically **retargeting** (people who already showed interest), not cold ads to strangers. That is how you get reasonable acquisition costs.

Your YouTube idea is actually not bad -- it is just premature. When you do launch it, you will create much better content because you will know exactly what your paying customers care about from all the conversations you had in Phase 4.

## The Bottom Line

You have $87/month in revenue. That is real and it matters -- it means someone is willing to pay for what you built. But 3 customers in 2 months is a weak signal, not a strong one. The worst thing you can do right now is spend money and time on growth channels before you understand why more people are not buying.

**Do the hard thing first:** talk to more customers, hear more "no"s, understand the objections, and iterate. When sales start getting easier and customers start referring their climbing partners, that is when you pour fuel on the fire with ads and content.

Revenue is the ultimate validation. Right now, you need more of it from direct conversations, not from ad impressions.
