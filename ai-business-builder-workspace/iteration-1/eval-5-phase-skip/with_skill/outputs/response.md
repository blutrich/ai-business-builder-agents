# ClimbClaw: Let's Pump the Brakes Before You Write a Single Line of Code

I can feel your excitement, and I love the energy -- wanting to build this weekend shows real commitment. But I need to be honest with you as a coach: **answering your tech stack question right now would be doing you a disservice.**

Here is why.

## Where You Actually Are: Phase 1 (Validation) -- Not Phase 3 (Build)

You said you have been "researching" the climbing coach market for 3 weeks. That is a good start, but research alone does not complete validation. Let me ask you a few critical questions:

1. **Have you talked to actual climbing coaches?** Not read about them -- sat on 15-minute video calls with them and listened to their problems?
2. **Have you confirmed that WhatsApp is even how they want to communicate with clients?** Maybe they prefer an app, maybe they use Telegram, maybe they want SMS. You do not know until you ask.
3. **Has anyone said "I would pay for that"?** Not "cool idea" -- but expressed genuine willingness to pay money for a solution like this?

If the answer to any of these is no, you are about to make the most expensive mistake in the framework: **building before validating.**

## The Mistake You Are About to Make

This is the single biggest trap founders fall into. They get excited by research, convince themselves the market is "huge," and spend months coding something nobody asked for. One founder in this exact situation spent months and significant money building a full product, only to discover customers wanted something completely different, forcing a total rebuild.

Three weeks of research proves a problem exists in the abstract. It does not tell you:
- Whether YOUR specific angle (AI coaching via WhatsApp) resonates with real coaches
- What the actual workflow pain points are (you might build the wrong features)
- Whether WhatsApp is even the right channel (this directly impacts your tech stack question)

## What You Should Do This Weekend Instead

Here is your actual weekend plan -- and it will get you to building much faster than starting with code:

### Saturday: Customer Discovery (4-6 hours)

1. **Build a list of 10-15 climbing coaches** to reach out to. Use LinkedIn, climbing gym websites, Instagram (many coaches are active there), and climbing communities on Reddit (r/climbharder, r/climbing).

2. **Use an AI assistant to write a customer discovery questionnaire** specific to climbing coaches. Focus on:
   - How do they currently manage client communication?
   - What tools do they use today?
   - What frustrates them most about their workflow?
   - How do they deliver training plans?
   - What takes up the most time in their coaching practice?

3. **Send outreach messages** and book 15-minute calls. Even getting 3-5 conversations this week would be valuable.

4. **Critical rule: Do NOT pitch ClimbClaw during these calls.** Tell them you are researching the climbing coaching space. The interview is about their problems, not your solution. You can share your idea at the very end.

### Sunday: Synthesize and Prepare to Launch (Before Building)

1. Take what you learned from any conversations you had (even 2-3 is enough to start seeing patterns).
2. Feed your research AND interview notes into an AI assistant and ask: "Based on all of this, what problem should I solve, for whom, and why would they pay for it?"
3. Use the answer to craft an **offer** -- not a product, an offer. What outcome are you selling?
4. Build a simple landing page describing that offer (use any AI-powered builder like Lovable, Bolt, or v0 -- this takes hours, not weeks).
5. Share it with the coaches you talked to and get reactions.

## About That Tech Stack Question

I am deliberately not answering it yet -- and here is the honest reason:

The answer depends entirely on what you learn from coaches. For example:
- If coaches want async training plan delivery, WhatsApp might be perfect.
- If coaches want real-time session tracking, you might need a different approach entirely.
- If coaches are already using a platform (like TrueCoach or TrainingPeaks), you might need an integration, not a standalone WhatsApp bot.
- If their clients are primarily on a different messaging platform in their region, WhatsApp might be the wrong channel altogether.

**Your tech stack should follow your validated understanding of the problem, not the other way around.**

Once you have completed validation and launched an offer (Phases 1 and 2), and you are ready to build an MVP (Phase 3), here is what the process looks like:

1. Create a lean PRD focused on ONE core feature -- the single thing that delivers the most value.
2. Skip specifying the technology stack in the PRD (let the building tool or validated requirements dictate it).
3. Ship within one week. If it is taking longer, you are building too much.

## The Bottom Line

You are 3 weeks into research. That is great. But you are probably 1-2 weeks away from being ready to build, not 0 weeks. The path to building faster is, counterintuitively, to slow down for a few days and talk to real climbing coaches first.

The founders who win are not the ones who code the fastest. They are the ones who validate the fastest. **Speed beats perfection, but speed in the wrong direction is just expensive exercise.**
