## What Your Validation Results Actually Mean

**7 out of 9 said they'd pay.** That's a 78% willingness-to-pay signal, which is strong -- most ideas don't survive this test. But here's what matters even more: the *who* and the *why*.

**Your segment breakdown tells a clear story:**

| Segment | Count | Likely Signal |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| Recreational climbers (3) | Probably 1-2 lukewarm | Nice to have |
| Competitive climbers (3) | Most excited | Urgent need -- they train seriously and want every edge |
| Coaches (3) | Likely positive | Could be buyers OR channel partners |

**The competitive climbers being the most excited is your single most important finding.** Here's why:

- They already spend money on training (gym memberships, coaching, gear, travel to comps)
- They have a specific, measurable goal (send harder grades, win competitions)
- They train with structure, which means they'll actually use an AI coach regularly
- Their willingness to pay is backed by existing spending behavior, not just enthusiasm

**Your ideal customer profile just sharpened:** Competitive climbers who train seriously and want personalized coaching to break through plateaus.

1. Gather all your notes from the 9 interviews

## Your Next Step: Phase 2 -- Launch (Before You Build)

This is where it feels counterintuitive. You are NOT building the app next. You are launching first. The goal is to get a landing page live, an offer in front of people, and real sign-ups or pre-commitments before you write a single line of product code.

### Step 2.1: Turn Your Idea Into an Offer

Your validated insight is that competitive climbers want a personalized AI climbing coach. Now turn that into an offer they can't refuse.

**Use this prompt with any AI assistant:**

> "I'm building ClimbClaw, a personalized AI climbing coach. My target customer is competitive climbers who train seriously and want to break through plateaus. In my customer discovery interviews, 7 out of 9 climbers said they'd pay for this, and competitive climbers were the most excited because [INSERT THE TOP 2-3 REASONS FROM YOUR INTERVIEWS]. Turn this into an irresistible offer. Then structure that offer as a landing page written like a direct response marketer. Focus on the competitive climber's pain points and desired outcomes. Make it dark mode with bold, energetic design."

### Step 2.2: Get a Domain and Basic Branding

**Actions:**
1. Check domain availability -- try: climbclaw.com, climbclaw.ai, climbclaw.io, getclimbclaw.com, tryclimbclaw.com
2. Grab one in the $10-15 range from any registrar (Namecheap, Porkbun, etc.)
3. For a quick logo, use an AI image generation tool -- climbing chalk + claw mark is an obvious visual direction

### Step 2.3: Build and Publish the Landing Page

1. Take the landing page copy from Step 2.1
2. Paste it into an AI-powered builder (Lovable, Bolt, v0, or similar)
3. For design inspiration, look at fitness/training app sites -- they tend to use bold typography, dark backgrounds, and action photography
4. Get it live. Connect your domain. This should take one day, not one week.

### Step 2.4: Start Outreach Immediately

**You already have your first leads: the 7 people who said they'd pay.**

1. Go back to those 7 interviewees (especially the competitive climbers)
2. Send them: "Hey, I took your feedback and built the landing page for ClimbClaw. Would love your honest reaction: [LINK]. And if you're interested in early access at a discount, there's a sign-up on the page."
3. Post in climbing communities where competitive climbers hang out -- climbing subreddits, competition Facebook groups, climbing gym Slack channels
4. Start booking more discovery calls with competitive climbers you haven't talked to yet

---

## The Mistake to Avoid Right Now

**Do NOT start building the product.** The most expensive mistake founders make is spending months building before they have a landing page, an offer, and real demand signals beyond interviews. Interviews tell you people have the problem. A landing page with sign-ups tells you people want YOUR solution.

Your next milestone is not "working product." Your next milestone is: **a live landing page with at least 20 email sign-ups from competitive climbers.**

Once you hit that, you move to Phase 3 (Build the MVP) -- and you'll build only the ONE core feature that delivers the most value to competitive climbers.
