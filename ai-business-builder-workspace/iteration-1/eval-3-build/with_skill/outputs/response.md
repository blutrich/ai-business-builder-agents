# ClimbClaw -- Phase 3: Build the MVP

Great news that you have a landing page live and competitive climbers showing interest. That means Phase 2 is working. Now it is time to build, but we need to talk about scope first.

---

## The ONE Feature Test

You listed five features: personalized training plans, session tracking, nutrition guidance, technique video analysis, and a coach dashboard. That is not an MVP. That is a full product. Building all five before anyone pays is the single most expensive mistake founders make in Phase 3.

Here is the question you need to answer honestly:

**If you could only ship ONE feature and nothing else, what would it be?**

Based on what you told me -- competitive climbers are showing interest -- the answer is almost certainly **personalized training plans**. Here is why:

- It is the core value proposition. Climbers come to ClimbClaw because they want to get better. A training plan is the most direct path to that outcome.
- It is the feature closest to money. A climber will pay for a plan that helps them send harder grades. They will not pay for a session tracker alone (they already use notebooks or spreadsheets).
- Everything else builds on top of it. Session tracking measures progress against the plan. Nutrition supports the plan. Video analysis refines technique the plan targets. The coach dashboard manages plans.

So: **the MVP is personalized training plans. Everything else is v2.**

## Product Requirement Document: ClimbClaw

### Overview
- **Product:** ClimbClaw
- **Problem:** Competitive climbers plateau because they train randomly without a structured, personalized plan adapted to their specific weaknesses, goals, and schedule.
- **Customer:** Competitive climbers (bouldering and sport climbing) who train 3-5 days per week and want to break through a grade plateau.
- **Core Value:** A personalized weekly training plan that targets the climber's specific weaknesses and adapts to their climbing goals.

### User Persona
- **Name:** Maya, 27
- **Role:** Competitive boulderer, V7 climber trying to break into V9
- **Pain:** She trains hard but randomly. Some weeks she overtrains fingers, other weeks she skips antagonist work. She watches YouTube videos but cannot translate general advice into a plan that fits her schedule and weaknesses.
- **Goal:** A structured weekly plan that tells her exactly what to do each session, targeting her specific weaknesses (finger strength, hip mobility, power endurance), and adapts as she progresses.

### MVP Scope

#### The ONE Feature: AI-Generated Personalized Training Plan

The app asks the climber a short series of questions about their current level, goals, available training days, access to equipment (hangboard, campus board, gym), and self-assessed weaknesses. It then uses AI to generate a structured weekly training plan broken into sessions, with specific exercises, sets, reps, and rest times.

#### User Flow
1. User opens the app and sees a clean onboarding screen: "Let's build your climbing plan."
2. User completes a short questionnaire (5-8 questions):
   - Current max boulder grade and sport climbing grade
   - Goal grade and target timeframe
   - Training days per week and session length
   - Access to equipment (hangboard, campus board, spray wall, gym)
   - Self-assessed weaknesses (pick from: finger strength, power, power endurance, technique/footwork, flexibility/mobility, mental game, antagonist strength)
   - Any injuries or limitations
3. System generates a personalized weekly training plan using AI, presented as a clear schedule.
4. User sees their weekly plan with each day laid out: session focus (e.g., "Power Day"), warm-up, main exercises with sets/reps/rest, cooldown, and notes.
5. User can tap any exercise to see a brief description and tips.
6. User can regenerate the plan if it does not feel right (with a note about what to change).

#### Screens Required
1. **Onboarding / Questionnaire** -- Step-by-step form collecting climbing profile data. Clean, one question per screen or a short scrollable form. Progress indicator at top.
2. **My Plan (main screen)** -- Weekly view showing each training day as a card. Each card shows the session focus, duration, and exercise list. Tapping a day expands the full session detail.
3. **Exercise Detail** -- Modal or expanded view showing exercise name, description, sets/reps/rest, and a short tip. No video needed for MVP.
4. **Regenerate Plan** -- Simple input where user can say what to adjust ("more finger strength work", "only 3 days instead of 4") and get a refreshed plan.

#### Data Requirements
- User profile data from the questionnaire (stored per user)
- Generated training plan (stored so user can revisit without regenerating)
- Exercise library: a curated set of 30-40 common climbing exercises with descriptions (can be seeded as static data or generated by AI)
- AI integration: the app sends the user's profile to an AI model and receives a structured training plan back

### Design Direction
- **Style:** Clean, modern, athletic. Think Strava meets a climbing gym chalk aesthetic. Dark background with accent colors.
- **Color scheme:** Dark charcoal background (#1a1a2e), electric teal accent (#00d4aa), white text, subtle warm gray for cards (#2d2d44)
- **Key UI patterns:** Card-based layout for training days. Step-through form for onboarding. Expandable cards for exercise details.
- **Tone:** Direct, motivational, no fluff. Speaks like a coach, not a corporate app.

### Explicitly OUT of Scope (v2 and beyond)
- No session tracking or logging
- No nutrition guidance
- No video analysis or upload
- No coach dashboard
- No social features or community
- No progress charts or analytics
- No payment or subscription system (for the MVP, it is free to test)
- No user authentication beyond basic email sign-up (keep it simple)
- No mobile app -- web app only for now
- No integration with wearables or other apps
- No multi-week periodization (the MVP generates one week at a time)

### Success Criteria
- [ ] User can complete the questionnaire and receive a personalized training plan in under 3 minutes
- [ ] At least 3 competitive climbers try it and say "this plan makes sense for my level"
- [ ] The plan is specific enough that a climber can walk into the gym and follow it without guessing
- [ ] Works on mobile browser without issues

### Timeline
- Target: Ship within 1 week
- If it is taking longer, cut the exercise detail screen and just show exercises inline
- Day 1-2: Onboarding questionnaire and AI plan generation
- Day 3-4: Plan display and exercise details
- Day 5: Polish, test with 3 climbers, ship

## What to Do Right Now

1. **Copy the Base44 starter prompt above** and paste it into Base44 to generate your first build.
2. **Iterate on what comes out.** The first output will not be perfect. Adjust the plan generation quality, tweak the questionnaire flow, and make sure the design feels right on mobile.
3. **Get it in front of 3 climbers within the week.** Send the link to the competitive climbers who showed interest from your landing page. Ask them: "Does this plan make sense for your level? Would you follow it?"
4. **Ship by end of week.** If something is taking too long, cut it. The exercise detail screen is the first thing to drop if needed.
5. **Do not build session tracking, nutrition, video analysis, or the coach dashboard yet.** Those are v2 features after you confirm people will pay for the training plan itself.

## The Warning

The most common Phase 3 mistake: building too much before going to market, then discovering customers wanted something different, and having to rebuild from scratch. You have five features in your head. Ship one. If climbers pay for personalized training plans, you will have the confidence and revenue to build the rest. If they do not, you just saved yourself months of wasted development on the other four.

When the MVP is live and at least 3 climbers have tried it, you are ready for Phase 4: Monetization.
